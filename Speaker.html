Human rights are fundamental rights and freedoms to which every person is entitled, regardless of nationality, ethnicity, gender, religion, or any other status. These rights are rooted in principles of equality, dignity, and respect. They encompass civil, political, economic, social, and cultural rights, including freedom of expression, right to education, and the right to a fair trial. The Universal Declaration of Human Rights, adopted by the United Nations in 1948, sets out these principles. Human rights protect individuals from discrimination, abuse, and oppression, ensuring the freedom and well-being of all people worldwide.
